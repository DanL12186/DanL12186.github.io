JavaScript has been rough. The biggest hurdle was probably the syntax; after having used Ruby for so long, looking at JavaScript again was a bit like reading over some all-but-forgotten language from some past life. The second-biggest hurdle might be in the error-handling... there sort of isn't any. 

I mean, okay. There is, and sometimes it's quite helpful; "unexpected ')', line:42:10 posts.js". But other times, there's sort of nothing. You're missing a curly-brace, or you have some syntax error, or you accidentally typed something twice or not at all, and you get thrown an error that in no way leads you towards what's actually wrong, and you spend 20 minutes trying to fix something that isn't actually broken while the actual culprit idly lazes around, wondering when you'll eventually find it out. Or, worse, things fail silently. 

Still, things have gotten much better. JS's documentation isn't so intimidating. As finicky as JS is, it's getting easier to work with, and some of its internal logic-- not always intuitive-- seems to be falling into place. While I greatly dislike the number of formats the language offers-- any one thing can be structured in a few different ways-- it's making more sense. 

This is, of course, thanks to being forced, once again, to work on a solo project without any help. As I've mentioned before, these solo projects with no support (other than Google) have been the things that have consistently consolidated my understanding, and this time was no different. 

It's been nice to integrate a little JavaScript (in the form of AJAX/jQuery) to an otherwise-static site. Simple as my implementations may be, it's satisfying to watch information that wasn't available on the page slide down or fade in out of nowhere. Historically, before I had any programming experience, I'd just assumed that these things were hidden by the page and then clicking on a link or button merely unhid them rather than actually sending requests and retrieving information from other parts of the site (or elsewhere).

I *do* wish Rails had more JS/jQuery integration features, although admittedly, I haven't really had a chance to check out remote: true, and there's a strong chance I'm missing a lot of what jquery-rails does. I always appreciate stable code being written for me and not taking up any space in my own code.  

On to React and Redux.
