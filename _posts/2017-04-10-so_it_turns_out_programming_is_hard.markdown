And I'll be honest, that isn't what I was expecting. 

For me, in general, when it comes to taking in new information and learning new things, the experience tends to fall into one of two pretty basic categories: It's either really, really easy, or really, *really* hard. There isn't generally a lot of in between, although it does happen occasionally. 

So, after a few initial humps (and an admittedly much-longer-than-I'd-like period of getting comfortable with reading rspec), when i found myself progressing rather quickly, and not having much trouble-- with one notable exception covered in my last blog post-- I was fairly optimistic that things would continue to get easier. 

Turns out, not so much. Enter Sinatra. The lab I (finally) completed today was as interminable as the sentence above this one-- which, at 327 characters, is the same length as the preamble to the constitution (coincidentally I promise). The lab before it took me two days, and I worked a full twelve hours on both of those days, by far the longest I've ever spent coding in a 24-hour period. (Actually, really the longest I've ever spent on anything in my life). Sinatra has been difficult for me; the combination of having to keep track of post/get routes (which I'll admit has gotten much easier) combined with difficulty understanding params, setting name=, and flat-out *not* understanding ids proved incredibly difficult, a far cry from the simplicity of SQL and the throughly-enjoyable ORM labs we'd covered only a little earlier.

Pry, a once-faithful companion capable of getting me out of any bind (Ruby puns, ugh), now seemed to mock me: ("Why does this work in Pry, but not when I execute Learn!? Where the hell is the variable I'm looking for? I have no idea where to look!"). 

I'd come away from one lab feeling I'd finally grasped one or more concepts i'd been struggling with, only to forget others, or to find it wasn't nearly enough (or both), because this new lab required things we hadn't yet learned. Googling, something I've rarely had to do thus far but had never failed me, often proved fruitless given how niche Sinatra is. 

It's been a very frustrating experience. I'm hoping Rails alleviates some of the issues I've been having and lets me leverage more of my strengths rather than hitting on my weaknesses; I'll just have to wait and see.
