Software, and computers in general are something that's been a fairly big part of my life for a very long time, and It's always bothered me that I didn't know how to program; as someone who's been a long-time power user and perennially frustrated with the limitations of the software I use on a daily basis, this felt like a particularly glaring inability.

A couple years ago I took an online EdX intro to Python course and really enjoyed it. More recently I picked Python back up on Codeacademy mid-last year. I finished their course in a couple of weeks and heard about CodeWars from a blog talking about ways to get ready for a program like Flatiron or App Academy. It proved very engaging, challenging and a great way to learn, and I pursued advancing in Python for hours every day. By this point I had already decided I wanted to pursue programming (which as mentioned is what had led me to CodeWars in the first place). I had been strongly considering going to a Coding Bootcamp, and spotted a Flatiron ad on Facebook for a free online course, and leapt at the opportunity. 

Though it isn't always easy (OOP is confusing!) it has thus far been extremely rewarding and exciting. I honestly can't wait until I'm doing it professionally.
